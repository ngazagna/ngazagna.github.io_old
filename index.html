Nidham Gazagnadou - Home

 

I am a third-year PhD student in the Image, Data, Signal department at Télécom Paris in France. I work on stochastic variance-reduced gradient and randomized iterative methods under the supervision of Robert M. Gower and Joseph Salmon.

My topics of research broadly encompass Optimization methods (especially stochastic variance-reduced gradient ones) for Machine Learning problems. More specifically, I am interested in extending sketch-and-project and splitting methods to large scale settings especially by analyzing mini-batch updates and by combining them with existing acceleration techniques (momentum/heavy-ball, Nesterov acceleration, etc.). I also try to establish links between existing stochastic optimization methods and reinterpret their updates.

As far as possible, I try to code efficient and reproducible experiments through tested open source codes. I am contributing to the Python open source package RidgeSketch (developed with FAIR New York) about sketch-and-project methods for the ridge regression problem. I also contributed to the Julia suite of stochastic optimization codes StochOpt.

I am already on the job market and open for a research scientist/engineer position starting in early 2022.

Advertisement : I highly recommend the BenchOpt Python package which aims at facilitating the benchmark of optimization methods for ML problems. I will try to contribute more to this package in the future.
